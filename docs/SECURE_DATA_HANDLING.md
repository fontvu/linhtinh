# Secure Data Handling Guide

This guide explains how to securely handle sensitive documents and information when using the Masters Tracker application and during your application process.

## Types of Sensitive Documents

The following documents contain personally identifiable information (PII) or sensitive data:

- **Passport scans** - Contains photo, passport number, date of birth, nationality
- **Academic transcripts and degree certificates** - Contains student ID, grades, institutional information
- **Employment certificates** - Contains employer details, salary information, employment dates
- **APS certificates** - Contains verified academic credentials
- **Reference letters** - Contains assessments and contact information of referees
- **Financial documents** - Bank statements, scholarship award letters

## Storage Best Practices

### Local Storage
- Store original documents in a secure, encrypted folder on your computer
- Use disk encryption (BitLocker on Windows, FileVault on macOS, LUKS on Linux)
- Keep backups on an encrypted external drive or encrypted cloud storage
- Never store unencrypted sensitive documents on USB drives

### Cloud Storage
- Use reputable encrypted cloud services (Google Drive with 2FA, Dropbox with encryption)
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all cloud storage accounts
- Share documents using password-protected links with expiration dates
- Revoke access to shared links after the recipient has downloaded them

### Physical Documents
- Store original physical documents in a locked drawer or safe
- Keep certified copies separate from originals
- When transporting documents, use a secure envelope and track the shipment
- Shred documents that are no longer needed (old drafts, expired certificates)

## Transmission Best Practices

### Email
- Use encrypted email (ProtonMail, Tutanota) for highly sensitive documents
- Password-protect PDF attachments when sending via regular email
- Share the password through a different channel (SMS, phone call)
- Verify recipient email addresses before sending

### University Portals
- Always use HTTPS URLs (check for the lock icon in your browser)
- Log out after each session, especially on shared computers
- Do not save passwords in browser autofill on public computers
- Clear browser cache after uploading sensitive documents on public computers

### Postal Mail
- Use registered mail with tracking for original documents
- Request signature confirmation for high-value items
- Insure valuable documents (APS certificates, original degrees)
- Keep tracking numbers and delivery confirmation receipts

## Document Verification

When submitting certified copies or translations:

- **Verify translator credentials** - Use only certified/sworn translators (thông dịch viên có công chứng in Vietnam)
- **Check notarization stamps** - Ensure all stamps are clear, dated, and from authorized notaries
- **Authenticate university seals** - Original university stamps should be embossed or wet-ink
- **Scan at high resolution** - 300 DPI minimum for document uploads to ensure text is readable

## Red Flags and Risks

Be cautious of:

- **Requests for documents via unsecured channels** - Legitimate universities use official portals, not personal email
- **Suspicious payment requests** - Verify application fees on official university websites before paying
- **Phishing emails** - Check sender addresses carefully; universities will not ask for passwords or payment card details via email
- **Document alteration services** - Never alter, forge, or misrepresent academic documents (results in permanent disqualification and legal consequences)

## Application-Specific Guidance

### APS Certificate
- Original APS certificates are valuable and cannot be easily replaced
- Request 2-3 certified copies at issuance time
- Keep the original at home; send only certified copies by post
- Scan the original at high resolution for electronic submissions

### Passport
- Your passport must remain valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended study period
- Scan the photo page at 300+ DPI in color
- Never share your full passport scan publicly or on social media
- Renew your passport proactively if it expires before March 2030

### Employment Certificates
- Ask your employer to include: full name, job title, employment dates, contracted weekly hours, company stamp
- Request certificates on official company letterhead
- Keep copies for your records before submitting originals
- If a company has dissolved, contact your former direct manager for a statutory declaration

### Reference Letters
- Referees should submit directly to scholarship/university portals (do not handle the letter yourself when possible)
- If you must carry a sealed reference letter, keep the envelope sealed and do not open it
- Store backup copies of reference letters in a secure location (in case of loss during transit)

## Incident Response

If a sensitive document is lost, stolen, or compromised:

1. **Notify the issuing authority immediately** (university, passport office, embassy)
2. **Contact universities/scholarships** where you submitted the document and report the incident
3. **Request replacement documents** as quickly as possible (may incur fees and delays)
4. **Monitor for identity theft** - Watch for unusual activity on your bank accounts or credit reports
5. **Consider filing a police report** if documents were stolen (required for passport replacement)

## Data Retention

- **After admission and enrollment** - Keep all application documents for at least 2 years (may be needed for visa renewals or academic verification)
- **After program completion** - Archive documents permanently (degree certificates, transcripts, employment records for future credential verification)
- **Destroy securely** - Shred or securely delete documents that are no longer needed after the retention period

## Questions?

For security concerns or questions about handling specific documents, consult:

- University admissions offices (for program-specific requirements)
- The [SECURITY.md](../SECURITY.md) file in this repository for application-level security
- Your local embassy or consulate (for passport and visa document handling)

---

**Remember:** Once a document is transmitted electronically, you cannot fully control where it goes. Always verify recipients and use secure channels.
